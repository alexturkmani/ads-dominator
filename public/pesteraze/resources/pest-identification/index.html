Termites (White Ants)

‚ö†Ô∏è HIGH RISK

Identification

Termites are often mistaken for ants but have distinct differences. They're usually pale white to light brown, 4-15mm long, with straight antennae and a thick waist (ants have elbowed antennae and pinched waists).

Physical Features

  • Pale white to light brown color
  • Soft, straight body
  • Straight, beaded antennae
  • 4-15mm in length

Common Species

  • Coptotermes (most destructive)
  • Schedorhinotermes
  • Nasutitermes
  • Heterotermes

Signs of Infestation

  • Mud tubes on walls and foundations
  • Hollow-sounding timber
  • Discarded wings near windows
  • Bubbling or peeling paint

Read our detailed guide: 10 Warning Signs of Termites

Why They're Dangerous

Termites cause more damage to Australian homes than fires, floods, and storms combined. They can compromise structural integrity and cause thousands of dollars in damage before being detected.

Cockroaches

Identification

Cockroaches are flat, oval-shaped insects with long antennae and six legs. They range from 12-50mm depending on species. Most are brown to dark brown, though some species are lighter.

German Cockroach

  • 12-15mm long
  • Light brown with two dark stripes
  • Found in kitchens/bathrooms
  • Most common indoor species

American Cockroach

  • 35-50mm long
  • Reddish-brown color
  • Can fly short distances
  • Often in drains/sewers

Signs of Infestation

  • Droppings (small, dark specks)
  • Musty odor in enclosed areas
  • Egg cases (oothecae)
  • Seeing live cockroaches at night

Rats

Identification

Rats are larger rodents with pointed snouts, large ears, and long scaly tails. The two most common species in Sydney are the Norway Rat (brown rat) and Roof Rat (black rat).

Norway Rat

  • 200-250mm body length
  • Brown/gray fur
  • Blunt nose, small ears
  • Lives at ground level

Roof Rat

  • 160-200mm body length
  • Black/dark brown fur
  • Pointed nose, large ears
  • Excellent climber

Signs of Infestation

  • Droppings (12-20mm, capsule-shaped)
  • Gnaw marks on wood, cables, food packaging
  • Greasy rub marks along walls
  • Scratching sounds at night

Spiders

Dangerous Species in Sydney

Sydney Funnel-Web

  • 25-35mm body
  • Shiny black color
  • Large fangs
  • DANGEROUS - seek medical help

Redback Spider

  • 10mm body (female)
  • Black with red stripe
  • Found in sheltered areas
  • DANGEROUS - seek medical help

Common Non-Dangerous Species

  • Huntsman: Large, flat, fast-moving. Harmless but startling.
  • Daddy Long-Legs: Thin legs, small body. Not harmful to humans.
  • Garden Orb Weaver: Large webs in gardens. Harmless.

Bed Bugs

Identification

Bed bugs are small, flat, oval insects that are reddish-brown in color. Adults are about 5-7mm long ‚Äì roughly the size of an apple seed. They cannot fly but can move quickly across surfaces.

Physical Features

  • 5-7mm long (adults)
  • Flat, oval body
  • Reddish-brown color
  • No wings

Where They Hide

  • Mattress seams
  • Bed frames and headboards
  • Furniture cracks
  • Behind picture frames

Signs of Infestation

  • Bite marks in lines or clusters
  • Small blood spots on sheets
  • Dark fecal spots on mattress
  • Sweet, musty odor

Ants

Common Species

Black House Ant

  • 2.5-3mm long
  • Shiny black color
  • Found indoors near food

Coastal Brown Ant

  • 1.5-2.5mm long
  • Brown/yellowish
  • Nests in soil or walls

Ants vs Termites

White ants are actually termites, not ants. True ants have elbowed antennae and pinched waists. If you see pale insects with straight antennae, you may have termites. Contact us immediately for an inspection.
